There are multiple jobs needed to run a musical, and musicals are always phenomenally better when you put effort into every second possible. For the WAMS musicals, there are about two months of practices before the performances. To make a musical, you of course need actors and actresses of all talents. But stage crew and faculty also play a major part.

To start, you need to have a director, otherwise it's complete anarchy. In some cases, you will also have a co-director to help. You also will have a choreographer, who makes the dances. Without him/her, you would just have a bunch of people standing around awkwardly while singing. You also need a sound person, so your microphones work come time for the show, as well as a lights person so the stage isn't completely dark. And, as it is a musical, there will also be a person to start the music at the correct moments. As you can see, a musical needs so many different people to make it work. Here are a few teachers who volunteered to make the WAMS musicals as amazing as they are.

Mrs. Schaubroeck: Director

Mrs. Schaubroeck has been teaching music for 23 years. Her bachelors degree is from Shepherd University (then it was called Shepherd College) and her Masters is from Shenandoah University. Mrs. Schaubroeck is married and has 2 adult children. She has been directing the musical at WAMS for three years now and has enjoyed every minute of it.

Mrs. Funkhauser: Co-Director

Mrs. Spielman: Choreographer

Brandy Spielman has worked as the choreographer for the WAMS musical for two years. She has a dance and theater minor from Dickinson College and has both participated in and choreographed numerous musicals over the past twenty years. In addition to theater, she also teaches ballet and tap at A&B Dance Dimensions.
